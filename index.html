About David

A lifelong resident, listening first.

David De La Hunt was born and raised in Hubbard County. Before joining the County Board, he spent 10 years as a Henrietta Township Supervisor — 8 of them as chairman — learning local government from the ground up. As an electrical engineer and licensed Professional Engineer who owns Normin Broadcasting, he brings a practical, numbers-first approach to county decisions.

His time on the County Board has focused on listening to residents, asking tough questions, and making decisions that balance growth with responsible stewardship of taxpayer resources. He also continues to serve on the Heritage Campus Board, where he helped stabilize operations after the COVID-19 pandemic and return them to positive cash flow.

Public service runs in the family: David's father, Ed De La Hunt, also served as a Hubbard County Commissioner and Henrietta Township Supervisor, and founded the family's broadcasting business back in 1962. It's a tradition of showing up for this community that David is continuing — and that his son Ryan carries forward today as a Captain in the U.S. Air Force.

Priorities

Where David standsDraft — needs Dad's review

01

Fiscal Responsibility

[DRAFT] Keeping Hubbard County financially strong means treating every dollar of taxpayer money like it's our own. "The purpose of a budget is to budget like you're fully staffed," David has said on the Board — pushing for long-term strategic and capital improvement planning so the county makes decisions with a multi-year view, not just a one-year budget. "Everybody has been impacted by inflation. So has the county," he's noted — and as the state pushes unfunded mandates and cuts down to local governments, he's been direct about where responsibility lies: "It's not the local governments doing it to you. The screaming needs to be pointed down to St. Paul."

02

Public Safety

[DRAFT] Rural counties depend on strong, well-resourced emergency response. David helped create the Walker Ambulance District to make sure coverage keeps pace with a growing county, and serves on the Association of Minnesota Counties' Public Safety Policy Committee, giving Hubbard County a voice in how public safety policy gets shaped at the state level. He supports keeping our sheriff's office and emergency services properly staffed and equipped.

03

Roads & Infrastructure

[DRAFT] Good roads are the backbone of a rural economy. David supports steady, well-planned investment in county roads and infrastructure so residents, farmers, and businesses can count on safe, reliable routes year-round.

04

Economic Opportunity

[DRAFT] Through the County Economic Development Authority and more than $12 million in secured grant funding, David has worked to bring real economic opportunity to Hubbard County. He was a driving voice in establishing the EDA, calling it "long term, money well spent" — arguing the county would "more than get your money back" through good planning. That includes tackling housing, which local economic development discussions have called "the 500-pound gorilla" standing in the way of growth — along with support for childcare access, another major barrier keeping local employers from growing.

05

Natural Resources & Rural Character

[DRAFT] Hubbard County's lakes, woods, and small-town character are why people choose to live here. As board chair, David led the effort to develop balanced short-term rental regulations, saying plainly the goal was "to protect all lake property owners" — not to punish anyone. He's also backed the Deep Lake Park development, working directly with state legislators on funding for its next phase — proof that growth and conservation don't have to be at odds.
